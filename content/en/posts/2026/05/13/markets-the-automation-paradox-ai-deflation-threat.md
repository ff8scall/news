## Strategic Deep-Dive

The Indian IT services sector, the historic backbone of global software delivery, is entering a period of structural instability characterized by 'AI deflation.' For the first time, the correlation between productivity and revenue has been broken. As generative AI tools automate complex legacy migrations and routine development tasks, the total volume of billable hours is plummeting. This is a direct consequence of the shift toward high-efficiency enterprise AI models, like those pioneered by Anthropic, which prioritize automation over manual intervention.

While headcounts in major firms like TCS and Infosys are largely holding steady, the 'revenue per employee' metric is under severe pressure. This deflationary spiral highlights the fragility of the traditional 'time and material' billing model in an era of exponential productivity gains. To avoid long-term decline, Indian tech giants must pivot toward 'outcome-based' contracts, where they are paid for the final solution rather than the hours spent building it.

This transition is technically challenging, requiring a complete overhaul of project management and financial reporting systems, but it is the only viable path as AI continues to devalue human man-hours.
