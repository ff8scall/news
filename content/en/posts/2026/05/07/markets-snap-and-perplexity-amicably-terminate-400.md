## Strategic Deep-Dive

Snap and Perplexity have officially announced the termination of their $400 million agreement to integrate AI search into the Snapchat ecosystem. The deal, which was a highlight of Snap's strategic pivot toward AI announced in November 2025, aimed to embed Perplexity’s unique 'answer-based' search engine directly into the popular social media app. This integration was intended to provide Gen Z users with a faster, AI-driven alternative to traditional search queries.

Despite the high expectations, the partnership has ended 'amicably' in May 2026 before it could be fully realized for the majority of Snap’s user base. The decision to walk away from a deal of this magnitude suggests a strategic re-evaluation on both sides. For Snap, the focus may be shifting toward internal AI development or different integration models that offer more control over the user experience and costs.

For Perplexity, the move might reflect a desire to remain platform-agnostic or to avoid the complexities of a $400 million multi-year commitment. This development is a notable sign that the initial rush to sign massive AI integration deals is being replaced by a more cautious, performance-driven approach in the tech market.
