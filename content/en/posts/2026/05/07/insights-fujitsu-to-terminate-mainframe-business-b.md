## Strategic Deep-Dive

Fujitsu has officially set 2035 as the definitive sunset date for its legacy mainframe business, marking the end of a multi-decade era that defined mission-critical enterprise computing. This strategic exit is not a withdrawal from the high-end server market but rather an aggressive pivot toward a new architectural frontier: Quantum AI supercomputing. By the mid-2030s, Fujitsu expects that the reliability and massive throughput historically associated with mainframes will be superseded by quantum-centric systems capable of tackling challenges that are currently computationally intractable, such as real-time global economic modeling and advanced material science.

The implications of this shift are profoundly geopolitical. Fujitsu is currently engaged in high-level discussions with the governments of Japan, the United Kingdom, and Australia to develop defense technologies aimed at 'contributing to global stability.' In the context of the evolving security landscape involving the AUKUS pact and Japan’s expanding defense role, Fujitsu’s transition is a move toward providing 'Sovereign Technology.' The future of national security depends on quantum-ready infrastructure that can withstand the cryptographic threats posed by adversarial quantum developments. Fujitsu’s roadmap ensures that by the time mainframes are retired, a robust, secure, and quantum-enhanced HPC infrastructure will be ready to take over the defense and intelligence workloads that maintain global order.

For existing clients, the 2035 deadline provides a clear ten-year runway for digital transformation. Fujitsu’s role will evolve from a hardware vendor to a strategic architect, guiding the migration of legacy logic into agile, AI-integrated environments. This transition underscores the necessity of replacing brittle legacy systems with quantum-ready architectures to ensure national integrity.

As we approach 2035, the industry will witness the birth of a new class of supercomputing that merges the precision of AI with the exponential power of quantum mechanics, solidifying Fujitsu’s position as a critical partner in the global security and high-performance computing ecosystem.
