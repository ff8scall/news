## Strategic Deep-Dive

During its earnings call on May 5, GlobalWafers revealed its tactical response to the volatile geopolitical landscape affecting raw material prices. Specifically, the rising cost of gallium—a critical component for GaN semiconductors—has prompted the company to prepare substantial inventory buffers to ensure supply stability. This move is part of a broader next-generation compound semiconductor strategy designed to maintain GlobalWafers' competitive edge in an increasingly unpredictable market.

In addition to managing raw material risks, GlobalWafers is focusing on the technical demands of high-performance computing and electric vehicles. The company is actively pursuing customer certification for its 12-inch silicon carbide (SiC) wafers. This transition to larger wafer sizes is crucial for improving yield and meeting the intense thermal dissipation requirements inherent in advanced packaging.

As GaN demand remains strong, the launch of a secondary sourcing strategy for these materials highlights the company's commitment to supply chain resilience. By balancing the immediate challenges of gallium price hikes with the long-term roadmap for 12-inch SiC technology, GlobalWafers is positioning itself to lead the compound semiconductor market despite significant external headwinds.
