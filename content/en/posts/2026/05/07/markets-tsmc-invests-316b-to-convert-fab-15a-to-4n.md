## Strategic Deep-Dive

In a decisive strategic realignment of its manufacturing footprint, TSMC has initiated a comprehensive overhaul of Fab 15A in the Central Taiwan Science Park. This 'Brownfield' conversion project targets a transition from legacy 28nm and 22nm nodes to high-demand 4nm production. The financial commitment is substantial, exceeding NT$100 billion (approximately US$3.16 billion), and covers the complete re-tooling of the facility with advanced lithography and etching equipment.

By opting for a brownfield upgrade rather than a greenfield expansion, TSMC significantly reduces the lead time required to bring new 4nm capacity online, directly addressing the sustained hunger for AI accelerators and high-performance smartphone silicon.

A key element of TSMC's capital expenditure strategy is its asset-circular production model. Rather than decommissioning the 28/22nm tools displaced from Fab 15A, TSMC is relocating them to global satellite sites where mature nodes are still highly profitable. This 'cascade' effect allows TSMC to optimize the lifecycle of its machinery while simultaneously upgrading its domestic hubs to serve the cutting edge of the market.

This move is particularly savvy in a high-interest-rate environment where maximizing the return on legacy assets is as critical as advancing to the next node.

Beyond the 4nm transition, reports from the Economic Daily News indicate that the 1.4nm (A14) timeline at the Taichung site is moving ahead of schedule. The acceleration of the 1.4nm roadmap is a profound signal to competitors like Intel and Samsung, suggesting that TSMC has achieved better-than-expected yields or infrastructure readiness for its sub-2nm future. By fast-tracking these 'Greenfield' projects in parallel with the 4nm brownfield upgrades, TSMC is effectively fortifying its moat.

The company’s ability to manage such a complex global logistics operation—moving mature tools abroad while simultaneously installing the world's most advanced equipment at home—solidifies its position as the indispensable foundry partner for the AI era.
