## Strategic Deep-Dive

The recent approvals from the Indian government mark a significant step forward in the country's ambition to become a global semiconductor and display powerhouse. By focusing on Mini/Micro LED GaN fabrication, India is entering a high-tech niche that is critical for the future of the display industry. The expansion also includes the development of large-scale packaging capacities, which are essential for completing the local semiconductor value chain.

These moves are designed to offer global manufacturers and buyers new, reliable sourcing options outside of traditional manufacturing hubs.

From a geopolitical perspective, India's deepening production capabilities in displays, power devices, and automotive components represent a strategic move to position itself as a viable alternative in the global supply chain. The focus on GaN (Gallium Nitride) technology is particularly noteworthy, as it is a key material for power electronics used in electric vehicles and high-efficiency industrial applications. As India continues to build out this infrastructure, it is likely to attract significant interest from global firms looking to diversify their manufacturing footprints and reduce dependence on a single geographical source.

This expansion is a clear indicator of India's commitment to playing a larger role in the global high-tech hardware ecosystem, transforming from a labor provider to a sophisticated fabrication hub.
