## Strategic Deep-Dive

The AI infrastructure boom has created a paradoxical financial environment for Original Design Manufacturers (ODMs). While their top-line revenue figures have reached historic highs due to the surge in AI server deployments, their profitability metrics tell a different story. The primary culprit is the inflation of the Bill of Materials (BOM), dominated by high-priced GPUs and HBM.

Because ODMs must procure these expensive components to fulfill assembly orders, their revenue is artificially inflated by the pass-through cost of the silicon. However, the manufacturing and assembly fees—the core of the ODM profit model—have remained relatively static. Consequently, as the value of the components rises, the gross margin as a percentage of revenue inevitably shrinks.

Beyond the margin squeeze, ODMs are also bearing significant operational and financial risks, including the capital required to carry high-value inventory and the technical complexity of integrating liquid cooling and advanced power delivery systems. This structural imbalance reveals a supply chain where value is disproportionately captured by upstream chip vendors, forcing ODMs to innovate in cooling and system-level architecture to regain financial leverage.
