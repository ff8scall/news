## Strategic Deep-Dive

## Capacity Expansion as a Critical Imperative in a Divided Market

Semiconductor Manufacturing International Corporation (SMIC) is taking a decisive stand to prioritize long-term infrastructure over short-term shareholder returns. In its Q1 2026 earnings call on May 15, the company detailed a major strategic realignment. By choosing to forego dividends in favor of a rigorous capital investment schedule, SMIC is signaling its intent to capture as much of the 'AI boom' as possible, regardless of the immediate impact on stock yields.

The company has identified the current period as a 'critical stage of capacity expansion.' With the global semiconductor market experiencing a significant bifurcation of the supply chain, SMIC is pushing to enhance its competitive standing by significantly increasing its CapEx intensity. This shift is aimed at ensuring that SMIC remains a vital link in the regional supply chain, particularly for applications that drive local AI infrastructure. The expansion is not just about volume; it is about scaling the specific technologies that will define the next decade of computing.

By reinvesting its earnings directly into advanced tooling and facility construction, SMIC aims to solidify its position as a leading global foundry while navigating the complex geopolitical and technological landscape of 2026. This data-driven investment strategy underscores the company's belief that manufacturing independence is the ultimate hedge against external supply shocks.
