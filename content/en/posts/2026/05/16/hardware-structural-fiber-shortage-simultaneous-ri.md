## Strategic Deep-Dive

The global optical fiber market has transitioned from its traditional cyclicality into a chronic structural shortage. Driven by the dense construction of cloud data centers and AI clusters, the demand for fiber—the core medium of data transmission—is outpacing global capacity. In a rare economic anomaly, both market prices and manufacturing capacity are climbing simultaneously, as vendors struggle to meet the relentless requirements of AI infrastructure.

With order books for major Chinese suppliers now extending well into 2027, the availability of fiber has become a critical strategic factor for the scalability of the global digital economy.
