## Strategic Deep-Dive

TSMC is intensifying its commitment to the United States semiconductor ecosystem, signaling a potential expansion of its investment footprint beyond existing plans in Arizona. At the US Department of Commerce's SelectUSA Investment Summit—where Taiwan maintained its position as the largest foreign delegation for the third consecutive year—TSMC indicated that the soaring demand for advanced AI infrastructure is driving a need for localized manufacturing. The acceleration of the Arizona chip expansion project is a direct response to the requirements of major North American clients who are leading the generative AI revolution.

By increasing its US presence, TSMC aims to provide secure, domestic access to advanced process nodes while simultaneously navigating the geopolitical necessity of a more distributed global supply chain. This move underscores a shift where proximity to AI software innovation hubs is increasingly outweighing the traditional cost efficiencies of centralized production in Asia.
