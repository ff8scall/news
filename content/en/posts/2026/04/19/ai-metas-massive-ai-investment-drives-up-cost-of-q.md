## Executive Summary
- Meta's aggressive spending on AI data centers is causing price hikes for "critical components," which in turn is increasing the retail price of Quest VR headsets.

## Strategic Deep-Dive
Meta’s pivot towards becoming an AI-first company is having unexpected consequences for its consumer hardware division. To support its massive AI ambitions, Meta has been investing heavily in data center infrastructure. This surge in demand has triggered a price increase for "critical components" shared between AI servers and consumer electronics like the Quest VR headsets.

As suppliers prioritize high-margin data center orders, the costs for specialized chips and optics have surged. Consequently, Meta is being forced to adjust the pricing of its Quest lineup upward to offset these rising manufacturing expenses. This trend highlights the internal competition for resources within tech giants and how enterprise AI growth can directly impact consumer pricing.
