## Strategic Deep-Dive
This article analyzes the accelerating threat of "Q-Day," the point when quantum computers can effectively break current encryption standards like RSA and ECC. While historical estimates provided a decade of buffer, new research from Oratomic and Google has shifted the horizon. Oratomic demonstrated that neutral atom quantum computing could break ECC with just 10,000 physical qubits, while Google’s research indicates that 1,200 logical qubits and 70–90 million Toffoli gates could crack 256-bit ECC in nine minutes.

Consequently, Google and Cloudflare have moved their PQC readiness deadlines to 2029. The synthesis highlights the transition from "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" (HNDL) protection to securing real-time authentication, noting the technical distinction of Shor’s algorithm providing a polynomial time solution to problems that take classical computers exponential time to solve.
