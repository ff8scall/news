This data illustrates the maturation of the technology. The goal is no longer simply maximizing the visual fidelity score, but rather maximizing the *efficiency quotient*—the ratio of visual gain to computational overhead.

## Future Trajectories: AI and the Frame Budget

The trend suggests that DLSR techniques will become increasingly sophisticated, moving beyond simple pixel reconstruction. Future iterations are expected to incorporate advanced generative AI models to handle complex elements such as:

1.  **Atmospheric Reconstruction:** Predicting and rendering realistic volumetric effects (fog, dust, rain) that are computationally expensive to simulate natively.
2.  **Dynamic Object Fidelity:** Maintaining high detail and low latency even during extreme motion, such as rapid camera pans or fast-paced combat.

For the gaming industry, the successful integration of optimized DLSR signals a maturation point. The technology has evolved from a "nice-to-have" graphical enhancement to an essential, foundational pillar of next-generation performance optimization, allowing developers to deliver console experiences that are visually breathtaking while remaining highly responsive to the player.
