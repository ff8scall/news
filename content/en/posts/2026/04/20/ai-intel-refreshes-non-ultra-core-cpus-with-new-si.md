## Executive Summary
- For the first time in several generations, Intel is bringing its newest silicon technology to the non-Ultra Core CPU lineup, allowing mainstream users to benefit from advanced architecture.

## Strategic Deep-Dive
Intel is breaking its long-standing tradition of segmenting its architecture by providing "new silicon" for its non-Ultra Core CPU refresh. In previous years, mainstream and budget-friendly processors often utilized rebadged architecture from older generations. However, this year marks a shift where the technological advancements found in the premium Ultra series are trickling down to the broader lineup.

This move is designed to strengthen Intel's position in the competitive desktop and laptop markets, offering improved instructions-per-clock (IPC) and better thermal efficiency across all price points. For consumers, this means that even mid-range CPUs will now benefit from the latest manufacturing process and architectural refinements, closing the performance gap between tiers.
