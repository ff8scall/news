## Executive Summary
The 2026 fitness tracker market is dominated by Garmin's rugged multi-sport watches and Oura's discreet smart rings. This comparison evaluates the technical accuracy of their biometric sensors and the shift toward predictive health analytics.

## Strategic Deep-Dive
The 2026 generation of fitness trackers represents a pivot from reactive monitoring to predictive health intelligence. Garmin’s high-end models utilize dual-band GPS and multi-wavelength PPG sensors to provide granular athletic insights, such as real-time stamina and recovery readiness. Meanwhile, the Oura Ring Generation 4 focuses on long-term biometric trends, utilizing advanced NTC thermistors and red/green LEDs to detect subtle shifts in body temperature that precede viral infections.

Technically, the battle is now fought in the AI backend, where raw sensor data is transformed into actionable health scores through complex neural networks.
