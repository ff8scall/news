## Executive Summary
A massive $6 billion AI data center project in a small Missouri town has triggered a local political collapse, with residents ousting half the city council. The conflict underscores the growing socio-economic tension between technological expansion and community preservation.

## Strategic Deep-Dive
The political turmoil in Missouri highlights the escalating "not-in-my-backyard" (NIMBY) sentiment regarding AI infrastructure. While the $6 billion investment promises economic growth, the technical requirements of modern data centers—massive cooling systems, high-voltage power lines, and continuous acoustic emissions—often conflict with rural or suburban lifestyles. This socio-economic friction is exacerbated by perceived lack of transparency in the approval process.

For the AI industry, this represents a significant bottleneck; the bottleneck is no longer just GPU supply or algorithmic efficiency, but the social license to operate in physical locations.
