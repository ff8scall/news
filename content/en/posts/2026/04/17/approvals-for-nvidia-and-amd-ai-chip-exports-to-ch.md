## Executive Summary
Export licenses for high-performance AI chips from Nvidia and AMD are facing significant delays due to a bureaucratic bottleneck at the Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS). A staggering 20% staff turnover rate at the agency is crippling the processing of critical tech exports.

## Strategic Deep-Dive
The stall in AI chip exports highlights a critical failure in the administrative infrastructure of U.S. technology policy. While the federal government has tightened restrictions on Nvidia and AMD to curb China’s AI progress, it has failed to maintain the human capital necessary to manage these regulations.

The 20% turnover at the BIS is particularly damaging because these roles require a rare blend of geopolitical insight and semiconductor expertise. This "regulatory bottleneck" is not just a policy issue but a technical one, as the lack of processing capacity prevents the timely evaluation of workaround chip architectures designed specifically for the Chinese market.
