## Executive Summary
Financial analysts have upgraded Tesla's stock rating, citing a significant market drawdown that has made its shares "cheap" relative to long-term AI and robotics potential. The re-evaluation focuses on Tesla's full self-driving (FSD) progress and Optimus scale-up.

## Strategic Deep-Dive
The upgrade of Tesla’s stock rating reflects a shift in valuation models from a pure "Automaker" to an "AI-Infrastructure" company. Analysts argue that at its current price point, the market is discounting the massive technical lead Tesla has in decentralized inference through its fleet of millions of vehicles. Technically, the focus is on the transition to "End-to-End" neural networks for FSD, which has simplified the software stack and accelerated performance gains.

This "cheap" valuation assumes that the robotics segment—specifically Optimus—will eventually represent a larger total addressable market (TAM) than electric vehicles alone.
