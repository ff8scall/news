## Executive Summary
Geopolitical instability caused by a potential conflict with Iran has disrupted expectations for U.S. energy dominance under the Trump administration's policy framework. The sudden surge in oil prices highlights the structural vulnerability of global energy markets to regional shocks.

## Strategic Deep-Dive
The conflict in Iran serves as a stress test for the concept of "U.S. Energy Independence." Despite massive domestic production of shale oil and gas, the U.S. remains technically and economically tethered to global spot prices, which react violently to Middle Eastern instability.

The failure of the administration’s vision lies in the inability to decouple local pump prices from global geopolitical risks. This shock underscores the need for a diversified energy architecture, including AI-driven smart grids and long-term storage solutions, to buffer against the "Energy Paradox" where being a top producer doesn't insulate a nation from price volatility.
