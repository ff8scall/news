## Executive Summary
As AI becomes ubiquitous, understanding core terminology like LLMs, hallucinations, and neural networks is essential. This guide provides technical deconstructions of common nomenclature to improve digital literacy.

## Strategic Deep-Dive
Demystifying AI nomenclature is crucial for professional navigation in the modern tech landscape. A Large Language Model (LLM) is technically a sophisticated probabilistic engine that predicts the next token in a sequence based on vast datasets. "Hallucination" occurs when the model optimizes for grammatical coherence over factual accuracy, revealing the underlying limitations of pattern matching without a grounding in objective reality.

Understanding terms like "Inference" (the act of running a model) versus "Training" (the act of building one) helps clarify why certain AI tasks require specialized hardware like GPUs versus edge-level processors.
