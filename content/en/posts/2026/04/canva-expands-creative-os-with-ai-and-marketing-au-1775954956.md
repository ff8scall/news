Canva, a leading graphic design platform, has made a significant move into the AI and marketing automation space with the acquisition of Simtheory and Ortto. This strategic expansion will enable Canva to offer a more comprehensive content and campaign platform, positioning the company as a direct competitor to Adobe and Salesforce in the enterprise market.

The acquisition of Simtheory, an AI startup, will allow Canva to integrate AI-powered design capabilities into its platform. This will enable users to create more sophisticated and personalized designs, further enhancing Canva's Creative OS. The addition of Ortto, a marketing automation vendor, will provide Canva with the tools to automate and optimize marketing campaigns, making it an even more attractive option for businesses looking to streamline their marketing efforts.

The acquisition of these two companies is a clear indication of Canva's commitment to expanding its offerings and becoming a more comprehensive platform. By pushing beyond design and into AI and marketing automation, Canva is positioning itself as a major player in the enterprise market. This move will likely have significant business implications for Adobe and Salesforce, as Canva will now be competing directly for enterprise budgets.
