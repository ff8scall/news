## Executive Summary
- The CEO and CFO of Fermi, a Texas-based startup aimed at powering AI data centers with nuclear energy, have abruptly resigned. This leadership vacuum creates uncertainty for the company's ambitious "AI campus" projects.

## Strategic Deep-Dive

Fermi, the high-profile startup co-founded by former U.S. Energy Secretary Rick Perry, is in turmoil following the sudden departure of its top leadership. The company aimed to disrupt the energy sector by building specialized nuclear reactors to feed the insatiable power demands of AI data centers in Texas.

However, the exit of both the CEO and CFO suggests deep-seated challenges in scaling nuclear infrastructure, navigating complex regulatory environments, and maintaining investor confidence amid soaring construction costs. This development reflects a broader cooling in the "AI-Energy" hype cycle, as the reality of nuclear deployment timelines clashes with the immediate needs of the AI industry.
