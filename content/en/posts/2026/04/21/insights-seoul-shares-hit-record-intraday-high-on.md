## Executive Summary
- South Korean stocks surged to an all-time high in April 2026, driven by record performances in the semiconductor sector and a heavy influx of foreign institutional capital.

## Strategic Deep-Dive

The KOSPI and KOSDAQ indexes reached historic milestones today, reflecting the robust health of South Korea’s tech-centric economy in April 2026. The rally was primarily fueled by the global demand for high-bandwidth memory (HBM4) and specialized AI accelerators, where Korean firms have secured nearly 80% of long-term supply contracts with Big Tech companies. Beyond semiconductors, the defense and biotech sectors reported exceptional export figures, boosting investor confidence.

Foreign institutional investors have poured billions into Seoul, viewing it as a resilient growth engine and a relative safe haven amidst the ongoing Middle Eastern instability. This surge in price-to-earnings (P/E) ratios suggests a fundamental re-rating of the South Korean stock market as it sheds the long-standing "Korea Discount."
