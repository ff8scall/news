## Executive Summary
- India faces a potential agricultural crisis as a weak monsoon coincides with rising fuel and fertilizer prices driven by Middle Eastern instability, creating a "double whammy" for food security.

## Strategic Deep-Dive

India’s agricultural sector is navigating a precarious "double whammy" in April 2026. National meteorological agencies have reported a severe deficit in monsoon rainfall, which is the lifeblood of the country’s Kharif (summer-sown) crops. Simultaneously, the escalation of the Iran war has sent global natural gas prices soaring, directly inflating the cost of the Haber-Bosch process used for urea production.

As India remains the world's largest exporter of rice and a critical supplier of sugar, any domestic production shortfall is expected to trigger a cascade of price hikes across global food markets. This volatility is further compounded by the Indian government’s potential fiscal strain as it balances agricultural subsidies with rising energy import bills.
